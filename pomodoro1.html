Focus To-Do

    Rethinking how to increase users willingness to manage their time with FOCUS TO-DO.

ABOUT

TYPE

Individual project for
usable design course

TIMELINE

3 month (Sep-Dec 2019)

TOOLS

Adobe XD, Pixlr, InVision

CONTRIBUTIONS

Research, BrainStorm, Wireframe, Visual Design

BACKGROUND

5 of my friends and I want to manage our time better, so we downloaded Focus To-Do, a time management app. However, within a week, most of us stop using it. Some people is not used to manage their time while others decide to try other similar app.

THE PAIN POINT

80% of users indicated that they had a certain degree of difficulty when they started to this app.


PROJECT GOAL

Redesigning the experience for Focus To-Do to raise the user retention rate.

DESIGN PROCESS

01. - Who are the target customers?

To understand the problems of people who needs time management I started with a lot of searching online, to understand what is struggleing them and what they care. To have a deeper understand I also interview 5 people with different background. Target users: a university student (junior/senior/ masters), a manager in a company, a career woman with a baby.

01 1/2. - Understand the market.

I first looked into the market to examine whether existing time management applications can help people better manage their time. I also observed whether they have any special solutions to attract users.

03. - User Journey

Before starting with the design and layout of our features’ wireframes, user journey is also an important part that needs to be understood. Through this step I can learn more detailed feelings of users from each step.

What are the problems?

Unfriendly experience when creating and starting a task timer. Lack of visual content and unfriendly flow design create obstacle to users. The main page was simply a list without any main functions, which differs from most users' expectations. The main two functions - create a task and start a timer, are in other seperate pages. The unintuitive design hurts users experience, which in turn leads users to switch to other time management app.
The theme color and style limit the recutment the potential users. During the interview, I had noticed that theme color and the app style are also concedered when deciding whether to use the app. Furthermore, the bright pink theme color for this app had reduced some users willing to use it.
Feel stressed to manage the time. Lack of consistency and motivation are two of the most common problems that stop people from pursuing a new habit. As a human being, it is natural for us to revert to familiar structures. Building a new habit usually often takes extra effort, and people tend to avoid effort. Thus, helping users to build up their time management habit is also a key to increase the usage and user retention rate. (As this is a personal project, I have no quantitative data to back up this hypothesis, and only qualitative data collected from user interviews.)

04. - Brainstorming for the solutions.

To provide a better user experience, there are 3 things that I need to improved 1）user flow 2) color theme 3) help users to build up a new habbit.
  • Reimagine users need for a time management system. Inefficient ways of presentations make the majority of the information displayed on the main page irrelevant to user needs. To provide a better user experience design, I moved the functions of creating a task and starting the timer to the main page, which helps users to use the app more intuitively.
  • Help users to conquer time management. There is a 3R principle (reminder, routine, and reward) for building and maintaining habits. The app already had the first two R's, so my solution focused on “reward" to attract and motivate users to keep managing their time with this app. I created a new coupon feature, letting users earn real-world discounts by accumulating their focusing time.
  • Redesign the style for more users. Redesigning the theme color and the whole style that is acceptable by more people.

05. - Sketches &Wireframe

With my research findings and personas in mind, I started to brainstorm and design a solution that can solve current problems and increase people's interest.

What main features have I chosen?

Redefined the countdown timer on the main page.

06. - Usability test

I followed Steve Krug’s template and KJ analysis as my usability testing. I am impressed by the feedback I got from the participants. It is fun to gain different perspectives from different people, especially when they are using the app and are trying to share their concerns for each step. Based on the result, I can learn what is their favorite part and what confuses them.

Detail Test Rrport (.pdf)

What designs have I changed?

  • Added a navigation bar and made the "add task" function appear on more pages.
  • Showed the progress to let users know the total estimated time they would spend on the task.
  • Removed the guide page for very-first-time users, since the design should be based on users' mental model without learning anything before they use this app.

FINAL DESIGN

Timer

Natalie now can see the countdown timer and choose the task that she wants to do directly after she opens the app. This page also provides the total estimated time for the task, so she knows how much time she will need to spend on this task.

Daily To-Do List

This list shows Natalie her daily tasks organized by category and listed from high priority to low priority. Natalie can choose to see other dates' to-do lists by changing the date on the top of the list page. Also, she can add a new task in one step by inputting simple information.

Coupon

With this new function, Natalie now uses this app to help her manage time. Although building a new habit is challenging, the coupon function also helps her to buy things for a cheaper price and saves money. This keep Natalie using it.

REFLECTION

What I have learned from this project...
  • Reframing problems
    In the very beginning, I thought this project is only an interface problem. I can solve the problem by providing a better interface design and user flow. However, after I talked with interviewees, I have found out that the core problem of current situation is more about human factors. Thus, I need to redefine the problem and find the real prolem that is behind the screen.
  • Giving up a normal solution for a better one
    To give up a solution that I already have, and to seek a better one is always hard. But since I reframed the problem, I need to throw away the old idea for a new to come. It is hard to do so, but the feedback from users let me know that this is the right decision.
  • Designers should not only focus on design
    Though I know this theory, it is hard to fulfill in reality. In my previous prototype version, the add button was on the main page, but since I don’t like that interface design very much, I removed the add button. However, after the usability test, I have realized how a big mistake I made.
  • Don't make users think
    I used to have a guide page in my initial version for the first time users. However, after the first usability test, I found out that users easily forget where to find the specific function. A good design should follow users' mental model and let them easily and quickly find the function they want. And also, although my app is based on a pomodoro technique, I should not think that everyone using my application knows it. I should apply the technique in the app without letting users' learn, and let users' gain the benefit from the technique directly.
