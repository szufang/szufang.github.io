Focus To-Do

    I redesigned this time management app to solve the disproportionate download rate and use rate, and to retain current users.

About

Type

Individual project for usable design course

Timeline

3 month (Sep-Dec 2019)

Team

Szu-Fang Wang

Tools

Adobe XD, Pixlr

Contributions

Research, Wireframe, Visual Design
Time management is one of the skills that many people want to learn and gain nowadays. Pomodoro* is one of the most popular and newest time management techniques. Recently, experts have combined this technique with the to-do list and calendar which helps the user not only manage the task time but also track their progress. The Focus To-Do App uses this idea to help people manage their time properly.

* According to Wikipidia, "Pomodoro technique uses a timer to break down work into intervals, traditionally 25 minutes in length, separated by short breaks."

Overview


Current Situation
Focus To-Do currently provides many useful functions such as analyzing users' time and letting them know what category or task they spend most of their time using. My friends and I started to use the app. However, we gave up a few days later after we downloaded it, because the application was not attractive enough for me to keep using it and to successfully help me to build up new habits. My project was to improve the app and retain more users.

(From left to right) Main page, add task page, and coutdount timer page.


Solution
I added a new coupon feature to attract users and motivate them to keep managing their time by using this application. By using this app, users can accumulate their focusing time. This time will be changed into points, and users can use their points to exchange for a real-world discount. Based on the user research and usability tests, I also changed the main color to black to increase the number of male users, and added the navigation bar to give users a more clear concept of what the app has and provided them a shortcut to any page they want.

Process Work


Step 1
Competitive
Analysis

I first looked into the market to examine whether existing time management applications can help people better manage their time. I also observed whether they have any special solutions to attract users.

(From left to right) Focus To-Do, Be Focus, FocusKeeper, Focus Time-Management, and Pomodoro with White Noice.

What fun facts have I found?

  • Besides Focus To-Do, other apps all use a countdown timer as their main page.
  • Focus To-Do, Be Focus, and Focus apps have a laptop version for people to use on their computers.
  • FocusKeeper has a timer and an analysis function but does not have a to-do list.
  • None of these apps have special functions.


Step 2
Interviews

After understanding the business side of other competitors, I started to focus on users. I tried to understand more about the challenge while people using this kind of mobile applications. I used the concept of Simon Sinek's golden circle to understand the why/ how/ what of interviewees' time-management needs.

What problems have I noticed?

  • Most of the interviewees want to have a better use for their time and to understand accurately the time they spend on each category or task.
  • Interviewees all have dificulty using time-management apps for a long time.
  • Some interviewees are too lazy to set up the task list.
  • To use this kind of app, interviewees need to be used to many new things such as key in the task they are going to do, start the timer before doing a task, and stop the timer after they finish it.
  • Theme color influences user's decision whether to use the application or not. The current theme color for Focus To-Do is pink, which stop many male interviewees from using it.


Step 3
Persona &
User Story

To better understand the target users, I synthesized the interview results and came up with the persona.
Based on the Natalie's goal and her needs, I also came up with user stories:
  1. Natalie wants to mange her time better so she can achieve her goal.
  2. Natalie wants to finish her assignment on time so she can get a better grade.
  3. Natalie wants to know the accurate time that she spends on each task so she can know what type of work should she put more effort.


Step 4
Journey map

Before starting with the design and layout of our features’ wireframes, user journey is also an important part that needs to be understood. Through this step I can know more detailed feeling of users for each step.

What opportunities have I found?

  • Main page should be replaced from a list to the countdown timer.
  • Information is not well organized. In the main page, the day of tasks and the category of tasks are putting in the same list. This makes users confused.
  • The app is not attractive enough for users to use it for a long time.

Step 5
Sketches &
Wireframe

With our research findings and personas in mind, I started to brainstorm and designed a solution that can solve current problems and increase people's interest.

What main features have I choosen?

  • Redefined the countdown timer as the main page.
  • Recategorized the information and Reorganized the list page.
  • Created a new coupon feature, so user can use the focusing time that they accumulated to exchange to real world discount.

Step 6
Usability test

I followed Steve Krug’s template and KJ analysis as my usability testing. I am impressed by the feedback I got from the participants. It is fun to gain different perspectives from different people, especially when they are using the app and are trying to share their concerns for each step. Based on the result, I can know what is their favorite part and what confuses them.

Detail Test Rrport (.pdf)

What design have I changed?

  • Added a navigation bar and made the add task function in more pages.
  • Showing the total estimated time on the timer.
  • Removed the guide page for very first time users, since the design should be designed based on users mental model and user should not need to learn anything before they use this app.

Final Design


Timer

Natalie now can see the countdown timer and choose the task that she wants to do directly after she opened the app. In this page, it also provides the total estimated time for the task, so she knows how much time will she need to spend on this task.


Daily To-Do List

This list is showing Natalie her daily tasks organized by category and listed from high priority to low priority. Natalie can choose to see other date's to-do list by changing the date on the top of the list page. Also, she can add a new task easily in one step by inputing simple information.


Coupon

With this new function, Natalie now is being used to use this app to help her manage time. Although building us a new habit is challenging, the coupon function also help her to buy things in a cheaper price and save the money, this attacts Natalie to keep using it.

Reflection

During this redesign project, I have learned a lot from my interviewees and usability testers. Besides the problem that I already knew in the beginning, interviewees had let me notice that there still are many problems that could be improved. To be more specific, the theme color is one of the potential problems that can be solved quickly but have a strong influence. And thanks to those usability testers, they pointed out my blind spots and let me know the problem of my wireframe design. Based on this usability test, I have learned that designers should not only focus on design but always need to put users' needs in the center. For my previous prototype version, the add button was on the main page, but since I don’t like that interface design very much, I removed the add button. However, after this test, I have realized how a big mistake I made. And also, although my app is based on a specific technique, I should not think that everyone using my application knows it. I should combine the technique without users' notice and apply it in their daily life. I hope this design can really help people not to give up while they are trying to learn to manage their time with Focus To-Do.
